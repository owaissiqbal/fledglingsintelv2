>  Ensure that apprentices who need to achieve functional skills qualifications are

### What is it like to be a learner here / What does the provider do well

> What is it like to be a learner with this provider?
>
> The proportion of apprentices and adult learners who achieve their qualifications is
> too low, and those who do achieve rarely gain higher grades. On many courses, the
> attendance of adult learners is too low, which results in them falling behind early in
> their programme and making insufficient progress towards achieving their
> qualification. Too many apprentices do not make sufficient progress towards
> achieving functional skills qualifications needed to complete their apprenticeship
> within the planned timescale. Too often, this lack of progress prevents apprentices
> from being able to complete their end-point assessment.
>
> When apprentices and learners attend sessions, they benefit from helpful face-toface and online teaching, which assists with the development of new knowledge,
> skills, and behaviours that they can apply in their job roles. Tutors explore real-life
> situations from the media when exploring areas such as safeguarding and British
> values. They use well designed and informative resources in these sessions, which
> often provoke fruitful discussions with apprentices and learners. In most instances,
> apprentices and adult learners produce work at the required standard.
>
> Staff effectively develop the confidence and resilience of apprentices and learners.
> This enables apprentices and learners to stay focused on their studies while
> balancing the pressures they have from their workplace roles and personal lives.
>
> Apprentices and learners feel safe in learning and at work. They know who to report

> communities. When concerns are raised, staff are quick to respond professionally,
> seeking advice from external agencies where necessary.
