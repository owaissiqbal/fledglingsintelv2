### What is it like to be a learner here / What does the provider do well

> What is it like to be a learner with this provider?
>
> Apprentices and adult learners have very positive attitudes towards their work.
> Equine groom apprentices enjoy their practical work and improving what they do on
> the yards. They enjoy learning the theory that informs their practice. Adult learners
> are motivated to progress into employment and self-employment at the
> end of their programme.
>
> Apprentices develop the professional behaviours they need to be successful in their
> future careers. Animal care apprentices understand the importance of maintaining
> high levels of animal welfare. They know the value of being reliable and trustworthy
> employees. Dog grooming apprentices take ownership of their work. They prioritise
> the welfare of the dogs in their care.
>
> Apprentices have a good understanding of the importance of legislation and the
> rules of law within their sectors. Equine groom apprentices can refer to legislation
> when outlining the importance of locked medicine cabinets. Animal care apprentices
> understand the importance of obtaining consent from animal owners to administer
> treatment or medication.
>
> Apprentices and adult learners acquire confidence because of their courses. Dog
> grooming apprentices become more confident when speaking to customers.
> Apprentices demonstrate resilience. Equine groom apprentices accept that they have
> much to learn in riding and horse management.
>
> Adult learners do not benefit from an effective planned personal development
> curriculum. Training consultants do not teach concepts outside of their qualification
> specifications. As a result, adult learners do not have the opportunity to develop
> their skills beyond the technical curriculum.
>
> Apprentices and adult learners value the inclusive and supportive approach at
> KEITS. Adult learners work in an inclusive, supportive and collaborative manner
> during training sessions. Horticulture apprentices are respectful of others during
> training sessions. They feel well supported and cared for.
>
> Apprentices and adult learners feel safe. They do not experience bullying and
> harassment. They know who to report any concerns to. However, too few
> apprentices and adult learners are aware of the local risks in the areas in which they
> work and study.
