### Main findings

> What is it like to be a learner with this provider?
>
> Too many learners do not attend well enough. Leaders and managers do not set
> sufficiently clear expectations for attendance to enable learners to develop
> successful routines for education. In too many training centres, such as in Yorkshire,
> the Midlands and London, staff do not successfully balance the challenge and
> support that learners need to help them access learning. In too many instances,
> poor attendance leads to learners leaving their programme early.
>
> Most learners and apprentices develop their confidence and resilience. In childcare,
> learners show persistence in dealing with setbacks, such as failing to secure a
> particular job, and keep going until they succeed. However, in the Yorkshire and
> Midlands centres, learners are not supported well enough to improve their
> confidence and resilience. A few learners find it difficult to adapt to changes in their
> day, including changes to class size or visiting guests.
>
> Apprentices develop new knowledge and skills and apply them successfully at work.
> Those on business administration apprenticeships develop their communication skills
> and their understanding of organisational policies and procedures, such as finance
> and data protection. Apprentices learn how to process purchase orders and approve
> invoices. They make positive contributions to the workplace and are highly valued
> and trusted by their employers.
