### What is it like to be a learner here / What does the provider do well

> What is it like to be a learner with this provider?
>
> Most young learners, including those with high needs, are overcoming significant
> personal barriers to learning by regularly attending their Green Labyrinth course.
> However, learners’ attendance is not high enough. This means that too many
> learners are not developing the basic employability skills employers require. Through
> low or irregular attendance, these young learners are not making the progress they
> are each capable of, or learning at the rate teachers intend. Young learners and
> learners with high needs are not experiencing consistently good teaching and
> learning or personalised programmes of learning.
>
> Too many of the young first and second-year learners, including those with high
> needs, have yet to access well-targeted work experience or work-related activities.
> This is a substantial gap in their expected learning experience. Leaders’ plans to
> introduce such activities and make sure they are all tailored to learners’ needs are
> new and it is too soon to gauge their impact.
>
> Most young learners are conscientious and focused on learning when they attend
> classes. They exhibit generally positive behaviours and attitudes in the classroom
> and study quietly to complete the activities and learning goals teachers set them.
> Most benefit from additional learning activities that help them to develop their
> resilience, explore new interests, and grow in self-confidence and character. Most
> are developing the skills needed to move on to either further training,
> apprenticeships or employment.
>
> Adult learners are very positive about the education and training they experience.
> They are ambitious, commit well to their studies, complete their work diligently and
> take pride in their achievements.
