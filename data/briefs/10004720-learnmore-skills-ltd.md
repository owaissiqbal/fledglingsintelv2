### What this provider needs to improve

> What does the provider need to do to improve?
>
>  Ensure coaches help level 3 early years educator apprentices to rapidly catch up
> and achieve their apprenticeships so they can move on to the next stages of their
> career.
>  Use the information gathered on apprentices’ knowledge and skills at the start of
> their apprenticeship to tailor training for apprentices so that all apprentices make
> the progress they are capable of.
>  Ensure that line managers attend and participate in reviews of apprentices’
> progress to ensure that apprentices can practise and apply at work what they
> have learned.
>  Ensure that coaches systematically coordinate apprentices’ on- and off-the-job
> training with apprentices’ line managers so that apprentices rapidly develop their
> knowledge, skills and behaviours.
>  Ensure that leaders accurately identify the weaknesses in the quality of education
> that apprentices receive and provide governors with this information.

### What is it like to be a learner here / What does the provider do well

> What is it like to be a learner with this provider?
>
> Apprentices on the level 3 early years educator apprenticeship are frustrated and
> unhappy with the disruption to their learning because of changes to their
> workplace coaches (coaches). They have not benefited from sufficient theoretical
> coaching sessions or regular meetings with coaches. Most have fallen significantly
> behind in their learning and have not completed their apprenticeship on time.
> Despite their frustrations, apprentices demonstrate resilience and motivation to
> remain in training and complete their apprenticeship to fulfil their career ambitions.
> The level 2 cleaning hygiene operative apprentices have had a much more positive
> experience. These apprentices enjoy their training, and most make good progress.
>
> Apprentices develop their confidence as a result of what they learn. Level 3 early
> years educator apprentices learn about children’s different religious backgrounds
> and cultural differences. Apprentices confidently apply this knowledge and
> understanding at work when helping children celebrate a range of religious and
> cultural events.
>
> Most apprentices regularly attend their training sessions when they are planned.
> Most develop occupationally relevant knowledge, skills and behaviours. Level 2
> cleaning hygiene operative apprentices have a secure understanding of the
> products and techniques needed for cleaning different surfaces, machinery and
> equipment. After completing their apprenticeship, most apprentices remain in
> employment. A small minority receive promotions or additional duties or move on
> to further learning.
>
> Apprentices feel safe, and most know how to protect themselves from radicalisation
> and extremist views.
