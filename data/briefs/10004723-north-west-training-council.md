### What is it like to be a learner here / What does the provider do well

> What is it like to be a learner with this provider?
>
> Not all apprentices benefit from consistently high-quality education and training.
> Engineering fitter apprentices have had changes of tutors and technical training
> assessors (TTAs). This has disrupted their learning and resulted in too many making
> slow progress. During classroom sessions, most apprentices are not challenged
> sufficiently to deepen their knowledge of key concepts.
>
> Where training is high quality, apprentices are motivated and enjoy their learning.
> They benefit from the industry expertise of their tutors, who they say inspire them
> to achieve their aspirations through the development of new knowledge, skills and
> behaviours. Apprentices take pride in their work and speak highly of the support
> that they receive from their tutors, trainers and TTAs. Attendance is high.
>
> Apprentices improve their confidence and communication skills because of their
> studies. They complete presentations in the training centre to reduce their anxieties
> about speaking in front of others. During classroom and workshop sessions,
> apprentices demonstrate respect for each other.
>
> Too many apprentices have a superficial understanding of fundamental British values
> and the risks associated with radicalisation and extremism. They are unable to recall
> this learning or discuss how it impacts them in the sectors in which they work.
> Apprentices who are under 19 years old are not given the opportunity to take part in
> discussions on topics such as healthy relationships and consent.
>
> Leaders have invested in industry-standard technology to prepare apprentices for
> employment and future skills needs. Apprentices benefit from learning how to use a
> range of resources such as flexible manufacturing systems and robotics. Apprentices
> develop the skills they need to become more efficient in their job roles.
>
> Apprentices feel safe. They are prepared well for the industries in which they work.
> Apprentices have a thorough understanding of health and safety regulations and
> apply these diligently at work and in workshops. For example, food and drink
> apprentices talk confidently about the need to wear personal protective equipment
> such as caustic suits when entering hazardous environments.
