> What is it like to be a learner with this provider?
>
> Apprentices enjoy their learning. They share their experiences confidently, and
> tutors create a respectful environment as others listen. Apprentices are eager to
> learn and develop the skills they need for their next steps.
>
> Apprentices learn new knowledge, skills and behaviours which they apply confidently
> in the workplace. Level 2 facilities services operative apprentices are given more
> responsibility such as analysing data and producing daily client reports. They
> improve their practical skills such as managing evacuation drills over time.
>
> Apprentices benefit from the positive relationships staff build with employers. Tutors
> manage review meetings skilfully so that apprentices reflect on their prior learning
> and set targets for ongoing on- and off-the-job training. Line managers understand
> the progress that apprentices make and what apprentices need to do in the
> workplace to develop their knowledge, skills and behaviours. For example, level 3
> team leader apprentices meet with and shadow engineers to see how risk
> assessments are created. However, in a small number of cases, leaders do not make
> sure that employers fulfil their commitments in supporting and enabling apprentices
> to complete and achieve their programmes.
>
> Tutors support apprentices well to develop the skills they need for their future.
> Apprentices are encouraged to conduct independent reading and challenging
> assignments. In review meetings, they are prompted to approach different people at
> work to get shadowing and mentoring opportunities. They learn how to overcome
> barriers such as shyness, persevere with difficult tasks and become more resilient
> and confident.
>
> Apprentices feel safe and are safe. They know how to keep safe online. Younger
