### Main findings

> What is it like to be a learner with this provider?
>
> Learners and apprentices work courteously and professionally with their skills tutors
> and peers. In online sessions, they adhere to the clear expectations set for them by
> their skills tutors. They keep their cameras on and take turns to speak so they do
> not talk over others. They attend these sessions well and arrive on time.
>
> Learners and apprentices are enthusiastic about putting into practice the new
> knowledge and skills they learn. They take pride in their work. Junior estate agent
> apprentices discuss confidently how to value different property types and how they
> would explain defects in a property to a potential buyer.
>
> Too many learners and apprentices leave their programmes early and do not
> achieve. Until recently, leaders did not place sufficient emphasis on the importance
> of learners’ and apprentices’ attendance at one-to-one meetings and reviews. This
> meant that leaders and skills tutors did not fully understand learners’ and
> apprentices’ progress and any barriers to achievement. Leaders have implemented
> actions to improve this. However, it is too early to see the full impact of these
> changes.
>
> Learners and apprentices feel safe in training and at work. They receive accurate
> information about the risks inherent in their job roles, as well as on matters such as
> sexual harassment and the risks of radicalisation and extremism. Most learners and
> apprentices apply what they learn confidently in the workplace. For example,
> pharmacy technician and optical assistant apprentices know not to share information
> online that may compromise their safety or patient confidentiality.

> What does the provider need to do to improve?
>
>  Rapidly embed the processes that leaders have established to ensure that more
