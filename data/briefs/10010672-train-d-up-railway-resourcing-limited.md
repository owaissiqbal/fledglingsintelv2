### What is it like to be a learner here / What does the provider do well

> What is it like to be a learner with this provider?
>
> Apprentices embody consistently high levels of professionalism in their roles. Train
> driver and passenger transport operative apprentices understand that they are
> directly responsible for the safety and welfare of passengers. They are aware of
> safety protocols and apply rail sector operational policies skilfully in line with their
> role. For example, train driver apprentices communicate critical safety information
> with signal staff during single line working and, as a result, they operate their trains
> safely and competently. Staff make sure MOET apprentices working for lift and
> escalator companies understand the risks to members of the public if they do not
> follow health and safety regulations.
>
> Apprentices have excellent attendance and very positive attitudes towards their
> apprenticeship. They are highly motivated to achieve and to meet the high
> expectations that trainers set for them. Apprentices focus well in lessons. They work
> collaboratively during group tasks and demonstrate excellent listening skills. They
> develop skills that employers value highly and they enjoy their apprenticeship.
>
> Apprentices value learning in a positive culture of mutual respect and care. Staff
> place a high focus on promoting equality of opportunity and diversity. This helps
> apprentices understand how to provide an inclusive environment for members of the
> public, particularly those with disabilities. For example, as part of their standard
> operating procedures, passenger transport operative apprentices provide special
> assistance to passengers with mobility issues to get on and off trains.
>
> Apprentices develop the knowledge, skills and behaviours they need to work in
> highly skilled roles. They benefit from being trained by staff who are specialists in
> their respective sector disciplines and use their knowledge and skills to plan and
> teach relevant learning activities. For example, rail trainers ensure that role-play
> simulations of emergency evacuations are as realistic as possible so that apprentices
> can apply and practise their skills. They challenge apprentices to prepare for the
> unexpected, such as passengers hiding under tables in smoke-filled train carriages.
