### What is it like to be a learner here / What does the provider do well

> What is it like to be a learner with this provider?
>
> Apprentices develop new knowledge, skills and behaviours well while completing
> their apprenticeship. For example, those on the lift and escalator electromechanic
> standard have a thorough understanding of engineering principles, components of a
> lift system and how to follow servicing procedures safely. Apprentices also develop
> skills such as problem-solving and effective communication. Apprentices apply their
> learning at work competently and make positive contributions to their company.
>
> Too many apprentices on the lift and escalator electromechanic standard do not
> complete their apprenticeship. Many leave their courses when they complete the
> national vocational qualification (NVQ) element of their apprenticeship and do not sit
> their final apprenticeship assessments. This contributes to the low achievement
> rates at the provider.
>
> Apprentices demonstrate confidence, independence and professionalism in their
> jobs. They lead tasks under supervision, which helps them to build their selfassurance and to develop their decision-making skills. Apprentices learn to work in
> different and sometimes challenging environments. They understand the demands
> of their role and adapt to new environments well. They interact with team members,
> managers and members of the public professionally.
>
> Apprentices are polite, respectful and motivated. Those who stay on their courses
> take clear ownership of their learning, reflect on their performance actively, and
> seek opportunities to develop their skills. They speak with confidence and pride
> about their learning journey and demonstrate maturity in their roles.
>
> Apprentices feel safe when in training, know how to report a concern and trust staff
> will take suitable action if concerns are raised.
