### Safeguarding

> safeguarding and the ‘Prevent’ duty.
>
> Staff provide learners with a good standard of teaching. Teachers plan, amend and
> sequence the curriculum well to match individual learners’ needs. They break down
> learning into chunks that are linked clearly to EHCP targets relating to
> communication, qualification aims, attendance and self-improvement. Teachers use
> effective questioning techniques to check learners’ understanding and encourage
> them to take an active part in discussion and group learning activities. As a result,
> most learners are progressing and achieving well, and attendance is now very high.
> However, achievement in functional skills mathematics dropped slightly last year.
>
> Learners receive a consistently good quality of careers education. Staff responsible
> for delivering careers advice and guidance provide learners with relevant,
> personalised careers information that is tailored to their individual aspirations. When
> learners have career aspirations that are either over-ambitious or not ambitious
> enough, they are guided appropriately towards what is realistic and achievable for
> them. Leaders have significantly enhanced the interactions that learners have with
> employers and have improved links with further education and training providers.
>
> Most parents are very positive about the support that their child receives. They
> value the way that staff are preparing their child for future life and education. This
> includes the introduction of a new reading strategy to widen learners’ experience

### What is it like to be a learner here / What does the provider do well

> What is it like to be a learner with this provider?
>
> Learners enjoy taking part in a range of well-considered visits and enrichment
> activities. They often go on outings to local supermarkets and tea rooms to
> volunteer in the community, visit historical sites and museums such as Beamish, and
> go on teambuilding activities through, for example, trips to Whickham Thorns.
> Through these experiences, learners successfully develop their confidence and
> resilience.
>
> Learners discuss a range of relevant, interesting and contemporary topics with their
> teachers that helps to develop and broaden their understanding of the world around
> them. These include discussions about well-being and news events such as the tree
> felling at Sycamore Gap. Learners also develop their cultural understanding through
> discussions about, for example, the Jewish faith and Rastafarian culture. These
> discussions help learners to be prepared for life when they leave education.
>
> Learners benefit from participating in well-planned work placements. Staff work very
> closely with employers and parents to ensure that learners receive the specialist
> support that they need to access their placement, including travelling to and from
> the placement on public transport. Learners with more complex learning needs are
> supported well by learning mentors and workplace ‘buddies’ who work alongside
> them to develop their skills in areas such as administration and catering. Learners
> understand the realistic opportunities open to them, including volunteering, paid
> employment and further training.
