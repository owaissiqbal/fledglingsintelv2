### Main findings

> What is it like to be a learner with this provider?
>
> Apprentices learn in a culture of respect, inclusivity and support. They appreciate
> their training, value their relationships with their trainers and enjoy their work
> environments. Level 5 operations manager apprentices work well with each other
> and their tutors. Apprentices treat their colleagues, clients and other apprentices
> respectfully and recognise each other’s diverse backgrounds.
>
> Too many apprentices leave their apprenticeship early. This is because many
> apprentices are unaware of the demands of working in the care sector and leave for
> alternative employment. In addition, a few apprentices achieve their additional
> qualifications but leave the apprenticeship before they take their final apprenticeship
> assessments. Apprentices who stay on their apprenticeship have professional
> attitudes to their educational development and employment. Attendance at work
> and training sessions is high.
>
> Most apprentices develop character and confidence as they undertake increasingly
> demanding tasks at work with support from trainers and supervisors. They develop
> the people skills of communicating empathetically and building positive relationships,
> which helps them develop the professional behaviours needed for their next steps.
> Most apprentices acquire a sense of self-belief and pride through their studies.
>
> Apprentices feel safe in their roles. Trainers and employers offer learning
> opportunities focused on health and safety in specific situations. Level 2 adult care
> worker apprentices who work with individuals facing mental health challenges are
> trained on safety protocols, such as working in pairs and using two-way radios to
> call for help when needed.
