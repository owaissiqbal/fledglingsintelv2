### What is it like to be a learner here / What does the provider do well

> What is it like to be a learner with this provider?
>
> Apprentices and adult learners are enthusiastic and motivated to learn. They are
> committed to their studies and demonstrate professional behaviours when at the PGL
> training centre and at work. Staff develop highly positive working relationships with
> learners that result in high levels of respect between them. Teachers know their
> learners and apprentices well, and due to small group sizes, work with them on a
> one-to-one basis regularly. As a result, learners and apprentices feel well supported
> and work at the pace that suits their individual need. Learners and apprentices feel
> safe and do not experience bullying or harassment.
>
> Apprentices and adults develop substantial new knowledge and the skills they need
> to move on to their next steps into full-time employment, and further and higher
> education. For example, level 3 plumbing apprentices learn how to slice pipes and
> develop an understanding of when to use copper or plastic pipes. Adults learn how to
> present the skills they have developed in previous jobs to prospective employers.
> This builds their confidence and enables them to apply for employment vacancies.
> Most adults studying on the short health and safety course develop sufficient
> knowledge to successfully achieve their Construction Skills Certification Scheme
> cards.
>
> What does the provider do well and what does it need to do
> better?
>
> Teachers are suitably qualified and experienced. They teach adults and apprentices
> very well, using their expertise skilfully to bring topics to life. Teachers combine
> theoretical knowledge and knowledge of apprentices’ workplaces well when

> learners’ understanding. For example, teachers on the adult employability
> programmes use careful questioning and coaching to enable each learner to identify
> a specific strength, such as organisational skills and caring responsibilities, and link it
> to an employability skill. As a result, adults and apprentices make helpful links
> between their workplace and life outside of training and this helps them to develop
> fluency and commit knowledge to their long-term memory.
